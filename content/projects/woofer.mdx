# Woofer

March 2023. The idea: transcribing and repurposing audio content into written articles takes about 10 hours manually. Woofer does it in minutes.

![woofer hero](/assets/projects/woofer/hero.jpg)

## the problem

Podcasters, marketers, and creators produce hours of audio content that never gets repurposed into written form — not because they don't want to, but because the effort isn't worth it. A 45-minute podcast episode could be a blog post, a LinkedIn article, a newsletter. It almost never becomes one.

## what we built

Woofer took three input types — uploaded audio files, YouTube video URLs, and live voice recordings — and produced clean, structured blog articles. Not just transcripts. Actual articles with headings, paragraphs, and flow.

![woofer features](/assets/projects/woofer/features.jpg)

## use cases

The two that resonated most with early users:

**podcast blogging** — record once, publish everywhere. Podcasters could drop their episode link and get a ready-to-publish post.

**marketing** — teams spending time repurposing recordings from webinars, interviews, and internal calls into content for their channels.

![woofer use cases](/assets/projects/woofer/usecases.jpg)

## pricing

We ran a freemium model — Basic (free), Professional ($29/mo), and Custom for agencies and larger teams.

![woofer pricing](/assets/projects/woofer/pricing.jpg)

## stack

Built on Bubble for the frontend, with custom APIs on the backend deployed on Google Cloud. Faster to ship than building everything from scratch — which mattered in a space that was moving quickly.

## what happened

Shut it down due to intense competition. The AI content space exploded in 2023 and the market consolidated fast around well-funded players. The product worked, but competing on distribution without a war chest is a losing game.
