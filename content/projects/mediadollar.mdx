# MediaDollar

December 2023. The data layer for BorderDollar — a startup exploring credit risk scoring for maritime trade finance.

## the problem

Credit risk in maritime trade is hard to price. Counterparties span multiple jurisdictions, geopolitical shocks move fast, and most of the signal lives in industry news — not structured data. BorderDollar needed a way to continuously monitor maritime and shipping news to surface risk signals for a future NLP scoring pipeline.

## what I built

MediaDollar is a three-part system: a scraper, an API, and a dashboard.

**MediaDollarDB** — a Python script running on a 6-hour GitHub Actions cron that pulls RSS feeds from six maritime publications:
- Splash247
- The Maritime Executive
- Ship & Bunker
- Manifold Times
- FreightWaves
- Hellenic Shipping News Worldwide

Each article is parsed, deduplicated by title, and written to a PostgreSQL database.

**MediaDollarAPI** — a FastAPI backend deployed on Vercel, exposing a paginated `/latest` endpoint returning articles sorted by recency.

**MediaDollarApp** — a React dashboard (Chakra UI) showing a consolidated feed of all articles with infinite scroll.

## why

The longer-term idea behind BorderDollar was to process this feed through an NLP pipeline — tagging articles by risk type, scoring counterparty exposure, and feeding signals into a credit scoring model for maritime trade finance. MediaDollar was the data layer: get the collection pipeline running reliably before building the intelligence on top.

## stack

Python, feedparser, PostgreSQL, GitHub Actions — RSS scraper and DB layer. FastAPI, Vercel — API. React, Chakra UI, Vercel — frontend.

## what happened

The scraper ran reliably on its 6-hour cron and the dashboard consolidated news across all six sources. BorderDollar didn't move beyond the prototype stage.
