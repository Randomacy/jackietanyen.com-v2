# Brik

Early 2025. An ESG reporting platform for Singapore SMEs — generate a tender-ready report in 30 minutes instead of hiring a consultant. Came after thirdLyr. Validated, found no wedge. Shut down.

## the problem

Singapore's government is tightening ESG requirements for public tenders. Large companies have sustainability teams and consultant relationships to handle this. SMEs in construction, logistics, and real estate don't — and they're starting to lose contracts because of it.

Three pain points kept coming up in research:

- **They don't know what to report.** Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions, Green Mark, embodied carbon — the frameworks are fragmented and industry-specific.
- **They can't afford consultants.** A proper ESG report through a consultant takes weeks and costs money most SMEs won't spend.
- **They're losing tenders.** Missing ESG documentation is increasingly a disqualifier, not just a nice-to-have.

## what Brik did

A three-step flow:

1. **Profile** — input your industry, company size, and active projects
2. **Upload** — submit utility bills, fuel records, spreadsheets, or answer guided questions
3. **Report** — receive a formatted, tender-ready ESG report

The whole process was designed to take around 30 minutes. The output was structured around the frameworks that actually matter per industry: Scope 1–3 for logistics, Green Mark compliance for real estate, embodied carbon and fuel-use reporting for construction.

To build this credibly, I enrolled in Coursera courses and got certified in Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions reporting.

## why it didn't work

This wasn't a case of insufficient research. I ran 30+ interviews with ESG managers, sustainability consultants, and SME operators — enough to confirm the problem was real and the pain was genuine. SMEs were losing tenders. The consultants were overwhelmed with demand they couldn't serve cost-effectively at the SME end of the market.

The problem was the supply side. Grnprnt and other established platforms had gotten there first — with integrations, regulatory credibility, and existing sales relationships that would take years to replicate. I could build the product. I couldn't find the wedge: the specific segment, distribution angle, or technical differentiator that would let a new entrant compete without those advantages.

Validation confirmed the problem was real. It didn't surface a path to winning.

## what happened

Shut down. Moved on to the next thing.
