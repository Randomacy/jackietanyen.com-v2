# Temasek Therapeutics

[download pitch deck](/assets/projects/temasek-therapeutics/presentation.pptx)

My first product. Built during my PhD at NTU's School of Biological Sciences, for the 1st Singapore Biotechnology Young Entrepreneurs Scheme (Biotechnology YES) — organised by A*STAR, ETPL, and Procter & Gamble.

28 teams. We made the top 5, went to the finals, and won **Most Original Award** and **second place**.

![temasek therapeutics presentation](/assets/projects/temasek-therapeutics/1.jpg)

## the product

**Lumina™** (露美™) — snap a selfie, choose your desired skin tone, get a personalised skincare formulation delivered to your door.

The core technology was a siRNA hydrogel in a pen applicator, combined with a machine learning model trained on a private dataset of 15,000 women. The app analysed your face, segmented areas by skin tone and freckle distribution, and recommended a personalised treatment regiment.

- **98% accuracy** in face segmentation
- **95% accuracy** in formulation recommendation
- Clinical trial across 150 women: desired skin tone achieved in **96.66%** of cases after one month

## the science

We weren't just pitching a concept — the IP was real. Two U.S. patents granted, two PCT international applications filed. The formulation had already been approved as a functional cosmetic by China's SFDA in August 2016.

We collaborated with **Prof Tan Suat Hoon**, Senior Consultant Dermatologist and Director of the National Skin Centre, to validate the clinical results.

## traction

The company received seed funding of **SG$921,888** in January 2015 under A*STAR's Joint Council Office grants. The target market was China's skin lightening industry — a $3.9B segment of the $23B global market.

## what i learned

This was before "personalised skincare" became a VC category. We were PhD students with a science-first approach, pitching to P&G judges who understood the formulation side. The "Most Original Award" felt accurate — nobody else was thinking about phone-based colorimetry for skincare at the time.

It also taught me that a good idea with a credible team gets further than a polished idea with a weak one.
